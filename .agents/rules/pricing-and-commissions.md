## Pricing Framework

CBC uses a **cost-plus markup model**. Each chef quotes CBC a per-guest food price. CBC adds a markup (20–25%) to arrive at the client-facing advertised price. The client sees a **single per-guest line item** — no separate service, coordination, or delivery fees. Add-ons (attended service, juice/boba bundle, etc.) are the only additional charges.

**Example:** Unique Cuisine's per-guest food price to CBC is $24. CBC's advertised client price is $30. CBC markup = $6/person (20%).

CBC is the billing entity on all orders. Client pays CBC. CBC pays each chef their per-guest food price × confirmed guest count within Net 30 of the service date. CBC absorbs credit card processing fees (~3%) on all orders — not deducted from any chef's payout. Sales tax is collected by CBC and remitted separately — not deducted from chef payouts.

## Quote Building Rules

1. Per-person client rate × guest count = food subtotal (single line item for client)
2. Add applicable add-ons (attended service +$8/pp, juice/boba bundle, premium packaging, etc.)
3. Apply 10% first-order discount if new client (100+ guests only; valid through 2026-09-30)
4. Sales tax collected by CBC on top of total — not deducted from chef payouts
5. Quote valid for **14 days**
6. Turnaround: **24 hours max**

> No separate coordination or service fee. CBC's margin is built into the per-guest price. Every quote should reinforce: one price, no surprises.

## Deposit Rules

- **25% of total order value** required to confirm booking
- Non-negotiable — no deposit = no confirmed date
- Applied to final invoice at time of payment

## Recurring Account Discount

- **NOT active at launch**
- Planned: 5% discount for standing weekly/bi-weekly orders
- Introduce only after chef agreements are signed and orders are flowing
- When active: discount applied to client total first, then CBC pays each chef their food price as normal (CBC absorbs the discount)
