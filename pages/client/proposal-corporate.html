4. Sustainability & ESG

Vendor Sustainability Summary

Culinary Block Collective works exclusively with independent chef-operators preparing food out of a licensed commercial commissary kitchen in San Jose, CA. Standard packaging is BPI-certified compostable. Chefs prepare food in small batches to order — minimizing excess production and waste.

Compostable Plates & Utensils (+$2/person add-on): For clients with zero-landfill goals or ESG reporting requirements, CBC offers BPI-certified compostable plates, napkins, and utensil sets on any order. This replaces all single-use plastic serviceware with certified compostable alternatives — available as a standing add-on to your program or on a per-event basis.

Carbon Offset Option: Available on request. CBC can source a verified carbon offset covering your program's estimated delivery emissions. Contact us for current offset pricing.

This summary may be used in internal ESG or sustainability reporting. For a formal vendor ESG attestation letter, contact culinaryblockcatering@gmail.com.

Inclusion & Representation: CBC's rotating roster spans Chinese & Southeast Asian, Japanese (sushi), Mediterranean, Italian, and Mexican cuisines — each prepared by a dedicated specialist chef. A rotating multi-cuisine program is a visible, recurring statement of workforce representation, not just a lunch benefit.

5. The Business Case

  • → Average cost to replace one employee: $15,000–$20,000 (recruiting, onboarding, lost productivity). Weekly catering at $25–$30/person is a measurable, visible retention investment relative to that baseline.
  • → Food satisfaction is a consistent top-5 driver in employee engagement surveys for tech companies with 50–300 employees. A diverse, high-quality rotating menu keeps the program fresh — not just "catering again."
  • → A recurring program with locked pricing and a 90-day review clause gives you predictable quarterly spend — one line item, no invoice surprises, no per-event negotiation.
  • → ESG-reportable vendor selection (BPI packaging, compostable serviceware, carbon offset option) supports existing sustainability commitments without adding operational overhead.

6. Program Terms

  • Minimum Term 3 months (one quarter), renewable quarterly
  • Review Clause 90-day review built in — adjust guest count, cuisine mix, service tier, or add-ons at each review with no penalty
  • Pricing Lock Per-guest rate fixed for the program term; renegotiated at renewal only
  • Billing Net 30 from event date. CBC is the billing entity — one invoice per event or consolidated monthly. Sales tax collected separately and remitted to the state
  • Individual Event Cancel Cancel any single event with 7+ days notice at no charge. Within 7 days: 25% fee. Within 72 hours: 50% fee
  • Program Cancellation Cancel the program with 30 days written notice after the minimum 3-month term. No long-term lock-in
  • Guest Count Range 50–500 guests per event. Confirmed 48 hours before each event
  • Deposit 25% deposit to confirm program start date; applied to first event invoice
