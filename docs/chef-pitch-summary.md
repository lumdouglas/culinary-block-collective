## What Is CBC?

Culinary Block Collective is a **corporate catering operation** built on top of the Culinary Block commissary. CBC connects our independent chef-tenants directly to Bay Area tech companies that need reliable, diverse, high-quality catering.

**You cook. We handle everything else.**

---

## How It Works

```
Client contacts CBC → CBC books the event → You get assigned → You cook → We stage & deliver → Client pays CBC → You get paid
```

### What CBC Handles (So You Don't Have To)

| CBC Does This | You Focus On This |
|---|---|
| Finding clients (cold outreach, LinkedIn, networking) | Cooking your food at the quality you're known for |
| Quoting, invoicing, and collecting payment | Sourcing ingredients and prepping to your standard |
| Client communication and event coordination | Handing off food on time at the commissary |
| Branded packaging, dietary labeling, menu cards | Confirming dietary accommodations are accurate |
| Delivery coordination and on-site logistics | Maintaining food safety logs |
| Marketing, social media, chef spotlights | Being yourself — your story sells |

Doug will also personally assist with **box preparation and staging** to ensure quality and on-time delivery. You're not doing this alone.

## The Money

### Revenue Split: You Keep 75%

| Client Price (per box) | Your Payout (75%) | CBC Commission (25%) |
|---|---|---|
| $20/box | **$15.00** | $5.00 |
| $25/box | **$18.75** | $6.25 |
| $30/box | **$22.50** | $7.50 |

**Minimum order:** 50 boxes per event.

### What That Looks Like Per Event

| Scenario | Guest Count | Your Per-Box Rate | Your Payout |
|---|---|---|---|
| Small team lunch | 75 guests | $15.00 | **$1,125** |
| Mid-size all-hands | 100 guests | $18.75 | **$1,875** |
| Large product launch | 200 guests | $22.50 | **$4,500** |

### Payment Terms
- CBC collects a **50% deposit** from the client at booking
- You get paid **Net 30 from the service date** (or within 5 days of CBC receiving full client payment — whichever is later)
- Payment via **ACH direct deposit** or check
- Every payment comes with a **remittance summary** (event date, client, guest count, gross revenue, your payout)

## What CBC Asks of You

1. **Sign the Independent Contractor Agreement** — confirms the 75/25 split, food safety standards, and mutual expectations
2. **Carry insurance** — $1M product liability + $1M general liability (you may already have this through the commissary)
3. **Provide a W-9** — required before first payout
4. **Finalize your menu** — exact items, dietary options, allergen info (we'll turn this into marketing materials)
5. **Communicate lead times** — tell us your minimum order size and how much notice you need
6. **Show up on time** — our on-time guarantee to clients means we need reliable handoffs

## What You Get

- **Paying catering gigs** you didn't have to find or sell
- **Professional marketing** — chef spotlight content, website profile, social media features
- **No upfront cost** — CBC invests in client acquisition; you invest your time and talent
- **Doug's hands-on help** — box prep, staging, delivery coordination
- **Flexible commitment** — you choose which events to accept based on your availability and capacity
- **No exclusivity** — you keep all your existing business outside CBC

## What This Is NOT

- This is **not a job** — you remain an independent contractor
- CBC does **not** dictate your menu — you own the food
- CBC does **not** take a cut of your non-CBC work
- There is **no upfront fee** to join

## Cancellation Protection

| Scenario | What Happens |
|---|---|
| Client cancels 5+ days out | You're notified; no impact to you |
| Client cancels under 5 days | If you've purchased ingredients, CBC reimburses your ingredient cost from the client's forfeited deposit |
| You need to cancel 7+ days out | No fee — just let us know ASAP so we can find a replacement |
| You cancel within 7 days | $250 cancellation fee (covers CBC's client-side commitments) |
