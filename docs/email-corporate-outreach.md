# CBC Corporate Outreach Email Templates
## Persona-Specific Cold Outreach — Office Managers & HR/People Ops

> **Purpose:** Updated cold outreach templates reflecting CBC's current positioning, pricing, and tone. These replace the Email 1 body copy in `docs/email-outreach.md` and should be used in its place. Emails 2–5 in the existing sequence remain valid as follow-ups.
>
> **Pricing:** $20–$30/person · **Minimum:** 50 guests · **Booking window:** 48 hours (under 150 guests)
>
> **Attach to every Email 1:** `CBC_Sales_One_Pager_v2.pdf`
>
> **Log every send in HubSpot.** Move to Quote Process immediately on any reply.

---

## How to Choose the Right Template

Ask on first contact or check their LinkedIn title:
- **"Office Manager," "Executive Assistant," "Workplace Operations"** → Use **Template A (Sarah)**
- **"HR Director," "Head of People," "VP People Ops," "Employee Experience"** → Use **Template B (Marcus)**

When in doubt, use Template A — it converts more broadly. Template B requires a longer sales cycle but targets larger, higher-value recurring contracts.

## Template A — The Office Manager (Sarah)

*Target: Office managers, executive assistants, workplace/ops coordinators at Series B–C tech firms, 75–200 employees.*

*Lead with: Reliability proof, not product features. Sarah has been burned before. Claims mean nothing — the guarantee does.*

---

### Subject Lines — A/B Test These

| Label | Subject Line | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **A1 (Recommended)** | `Catering that doesn't require babysitting the morning of` | Cold list, no prior contact |
| A2 | `The Bay Area caterer your team will actually request by name` | If they've visited the website |
| A3 | `Quick question about catering at [Company]` | High-volume sends where personalization is limited |
| A4 | `[Company] team lunch — one thing most caterers won't put in writing` | High-intent prospects, follow up to a LinkedIn connect |

> **A/B testing guidance:** Run A1 vs. A3 on your first 50 sends. A1 gets lower open rate but higher reply quality. A3 gets higher opens but more "not interested" replies. Track in HubSpot.

### Email Body

**To:** [First Name] [Last Name] · [Office Manager / Workplace Ops / EA] · [Company]

---

Hi [First Name],

Here's the thing about corporate catering in the Bay Area: every vendor calls themselves reliable — right up until the food shows up wrong at a 120-person all-hands.

Culinary Block Collective is different in one specific way: our on-time delivery guarantee is in writing. **If your food isn't staged and ready for your team at the agreed time, your next order is automatically 10% off.**

We're a San Jose-based catering operation built around independent specialist chefs — each one trained in their cuisine specifically, not a generalist covering every station. Your team gets sushi from a sushi chef, Chinese from a Chinese chef. Food people actually get excited about.

**What that means for [Company]:**

We're booking **free tastings** for new corporate accounts this month — no commitment, just food. A handful of slots remain.

Worth a 15-minute call this week? Or text me directly at [phone] — happy to keep it that short.

[Your Name]
Culinary Block Collective | San Jose, CA
[Phone] · hello@culinaryblockcollective.com · culinaryblockcollective.com

*P.S. — If you're currently locked into another vendor, no problem. Let me know when that contract renews and I'll reach back out closer to that date.*

### Personalization Notes — Template A

| Field | Where to find it | What to customize |
|---|---|---|
| `[Company]` | HubSpot lead record | Use in subject + body |
| `[First Name]` | LinkedIn / HubSpot | Always use — "Hi there" tanks open rates |
| Guest count reference | Company size from LinkedIn | If they're 50–80 people, adjust "120-person" reference downward so it feels real |
| Cuisine mention | Their LinkedIn or company culture page | If they post about specific cultures on their team, name a matching cuisine ("We have a Chinese cuisine specialist on our roster — not a lot of caterers in the Bay can say that") |
| Pain reference | Prior vendor reviews on Google/Yelp if visible | Reference their city: "We work with a lot of teams in Mountain View" |

---

### What Not to Do — Template A

- **Don't lead with the menu.** Sarah doesn't care about the menu until she trusts the vendor.
- **Don't say "in-house chefs."** They're independent specialists — use that language.
- **Don't oversell the variety upfront.** One sentence on cuisine diversity is enough. The guarantee is the hook.
- **Don't attach the full menu PDF to Email 1.** Save it for Email 3 (menu drop) — it creates a reason to follow up.

## Template B — The HR/People Ops Director (Marcus)

*Target: HR Directors, Heads of People, VP People Ops, Employee Experience leads at Series C–D or post-IPO tech firms, 150–300 employees.*

*Lead with: Culture representation and program ROI — not just a one-time lunch. Marcus is building a program, not booking an event. He needs a number he can take to the CFO.*

---

### Subject Lines — A/B Test These

| Label | Subject Line | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **B1 (Recommended)** | `Your catering vendor is a culture statement — does yours reflect your team?` | Cold LinkedIn DM or email |
| B2 | `The People Ops angle on corporate catering most vendors miss` | LinkedIn InMail after engaging with their content |
| B3 | `[Company]'s food program — quick question` | High-volume sends |
| B4 | `ESG-reportable catering + eNPS ROI — worth 15 minutes?` | If company has published ESG commitments |

> **Tip:** For Marcus, LinkedIn DM → Email works better than Email alone. Send a connection request referencing something specific about their company (ESG report, headcount growth announcement, culture award) before sending this email. Reference it in the opening line.

[Your Name]
Culinary Block Collective | San Jose, CA
[Phone] · hello@culinaryblockcollective.com · culinaryblockcollective.com

*P.S. — If your current vendor is under contract, ask me when it renews. I'll stay warm with relevant content and reach back out 60 days before renewal.*

### Personalization Notes — Template B

| Field | Where to find it | What to customize |
|---|---|---|
| Nationality reference | Company LinkedIn / Glassdoor / "About" page | Be specific if they publish DEI data: "your team spans 18 nationalities" lands harder than a generic reference |
| ESG mention | Company website / annual report / press | If they've published ESG targets, name them: "Given [Company]'s published sustainability commitments..." |
| Headcount | LinkedIn company page | If 150–200, emphasize scalability; if 200–300, emphasize existing scale capability |
| Budget framing | LinkedIn job postings (look for "perks" language) | If they offer free meals as a listed perk, they're already spending — frame as a quality upgrade, not a new cost |
| Decision trigger | Recent company news | Fundraise → new headcount → new food program need. Reference it: "Congrats on the Series C — teams tend to revisit vendors as they scale." |

---

### What Not to Do — Template B

- **Don't pitch the price per person first.** Marcus thinks in quarterly budget, not per-box price.
- **Don't send the full menu.** Send a program proposal instead — it matches how he thinks.
- **Don't start with the delivery guarantee.** That's a Sarah hook. Marcus leads with culture and ROI.
- **Don't use "we're reliable."** Substantiate it: "On-time delivery guaranteed in writing" is the same thing, said credibly.
