# CBC Sales Growth Plan
## 30 · 60 · 90 Day Playbook | Bay Area Corporate Catering | 2025

> **Source:** `CBC_Sales_Action_Plan_30_60_90.docx` (converted to markdown)

## Sales North Star

Everything in this plan serves one outcome: **consistent, growing revenue from Bay Area corporate catering clients.** The fastest path is direct outreach to the right decision-maker, a phone/text-first close, and converting one-time orders into recurring contracts.

### 6-Month Targets

| Metric | Month 3 Target | Month 6 Target |
|---|---|---|
| Paying clients | 5–8 | 15+ |
| Recurring contracts | 1–2 | 5+ |
| Avg Order Value | $2,000+ | $2,500+ |
| Revenue | $10K–$15K | $28K–$35K |
| Leads in pipeline | 30+ | 75+ |

## Days 1–30: Foundation & First Wins

*Install the sales machine and land the first 2–3 paying clients fast.*

### Week 1 — Set Up to Sell
**Goal:** Website live, phone trackable, CRM loaded, first 25 leads identified.

**Monday–Tuesday: Sales Infrastructure**
- Add call tracking to Squarespace — use CallRail ($45/mo) or Google Call Tracking
- Set up HubSpot CRM (free) — pipeline stages: New Lead → Contacted → Quote Sent → Won → Recurring
- Install Google Analytics 4 on Squarespace (Settings > Advanced > Code Injection)
- Set up Google Business Profile at business.google.com — drives local SEO and direct calls

**Wednesday–Thursday: Load First 25 Leads**
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator filter: Job Title = 'Office Manager' OR 'Executive Assistant' OR 'HR' | Location: Bay Area | Company size: 50–500
- Export 25 contacts into HubSpot — add company name, LinkedIn URL, personal email if visible
- Research each company: funding stage, recent news, team size

**Friday: Launch Day**
- Send Email 1 (cold intro + one-pager) to all 25 contacts
- Post LinkedIn launch post — "We just launched corporate catering for Bay Area tech teams"
- Text or DM 5 warm contacts personally — not the email template

---

### Week 2 — Outreach Blitz
**Goal:** 25 more leads contacted. 3–5 replies. 1 consultation booked.

**Daily Rhythm (1 hour/day)**
- Morning (20 min): Check HubSpot — follow up on any Email 1 opens/clicks from previous day
- Mid-day (20 min): Send 5 new personalized LinkedIn connection requests with custom note
- Afternoon (20 min): Post or engage on LinkedIn — comment on a prospect's company post

**Monday — Follow-Up Wave**
- Send Email 2 (value follow-up) to the 25 from Week 1 who haven't replied
- Call any prospect who opened your email but didn't reply — open = warm
- Script: *"Hi [Name], I sent over some info on our chef catering last week — did you get a chance to look? I'd love to find out if it's a fit for [Company]."*

**Wednesday — LinkedIn DM Push**
- Message 25 new connections directly — 2 sentences: what you do + 1 specific hook
- *"Hi [Name] — we do diverse chef catering for tech teams in the Bay Area (sushi/Japanese, Chinese, Mexican fusion). Curious if [Company] uses external caterers for team lunches?"*

**Friday — Free Tasting Invite**
- Send personal invite to the 5 most engaged prospects for the free chef tasting
- Post an Instagram Story: "2 tasting slots left this month — DM us"

---

### Week 3 — Close First Clients
**Goal:** 1–2 first orders confirmed. Quote sent to 5+ prospects.

- Send Email 3 (menu drop + 10% offer) to all non-responders
- For anyone who replied: send a custom quote within 2 hours — **speed to quote is your biggest conversion lever**
- Book free Zoom consultations for interested prospects — use Calendly (free)
- Set up Squarespace booking form fields: Date, Guest Count, Cuisine Preference, Dietary Needs, Budget, Best time to call

---

### Week 4 — Optimize & Scale
**Goal:** Review what worked. Double down. Add 25 more leads.

- Review HubSpot: Which email subject line had the best open rate?
- Review call tracking: Which page/channel drove the most inbound calls?
- Review LinkedIn: Which message got the most replies?
- Add 25 new leads — target a different segment (e.g., HR Directors instead of Office Managers)
- Ask your first client for a Google review and a LinkedIn testimonial

  - 'How to Choose a Corporate Caterer in the Bay Area'
  - 'Best Office Lunch Catering Options in San Jose 2025'
  - 'Healthy Corporate Catering for Tech Teams: What to Look For'

### Weeks 7–8: Conversion Rate Optimization
**Goal:** Increase quote-to-close rate from ~10% to 20%+.

**Speed is your #1 conversion lever**
- Respond to every form fill, email reply, or call within 2 hours — 78% of clients go with the first vendor to respond
- Send a voice memo (via iMessage/WhatsApp) instead of a text — more personal and memorable
- Outbound text opener: *"Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Culinary Block — I saw you reached out about catering for [Company]. Mind if I ask a couple quick questions to get you the right quote?"*

**Quote Improvements**
- Add a photo of one signature dish to every quote email — visual converts
- Include a scarcity line: *"We only have 3 delivery slots available in [Month] — happy to hold one for you"*
- Offer two options in every quote (not one) — changes conversation from 'yes or no' to 'which one'

## Days 61–90: Scale & Recurring Revenue

*Convert the best one-time clients into recurring weekly accounts — your most valuable revenue.*

### Recurring Contract Push
**Goal:** 5 recurring weekly or bi-weekly accounts by Day 90.

- After every completed order: send Email 5 (post-event thank you + recurring offer) within 48 hours
- Call top 3 clients personally — not email. Script: *"We loved catering for your team. Have you thought about a standing weekly order? We'll lock in your rate with fixed pricing and rotating menus."*
- Create a 'Recurring Catering Proposal' — one-page PDF: weekly menu rotation, locked per-head pricing, 90-day review clause, no cancellation fee for 30 days
- Offer 3-month minimum — enough to build routine, low enough to feel safe

### Referral Machine
**Goal:** Every happy client becomes a referral source.

- Ask explicitly: *"Do you know any other office managers in the area? We're accepting 2–3 new corporate clients this month."*
- Create referral program: 10% credit on next order for every referral that places an order
- Send a 'Refer a Friend' card (physical or digital) with every delivered order
- Ask every client to post about their event on LinkedIn and tag Culinary Block

### Channel Expansion (Month 3)

**LinkedIn Paid Ads**
- Sponsored Content: $200/month, targeting Office Managers + HR at 50–500 person tech companies in Bay Area
- Ad creative: chef photo + "Your team's favorite lunch caterer — call (408) 555-0192"
- Track calls using CallRail — pause anything that doesn't drive a call within 2 weeks

**Instagram Reels**
- 2 Reels/week: food prep, spread setup, happy team photos
- Location tags: #SanJoseCatering #BayAreaCatering #TechLunch #SiliconValleyFood
- Giveaway: "Tag an office manager who deserves great catering — winner gets a free tasting for their team"

**Google Ads (Optional)**
- Keyword: 'corporate catering san jose' — Max CPC $4–6, daily budget $10–15
- Ad headline: 'Corporate Catering San Jose | 6 Chef Cuisines | Call Now'
- Only run if Google Business organic is not producing 5+ calls/week by Day 60

## Phone & Text Sales Scripts

### Inbound Call Script (They Called You)

**Open:**
> "Thanks for calling Culinary Block, this is [Name] — are you looking to book catering for your team?"

**Discovery Questions (Ask All 5):**
1. "What's the occasion — team lunch, client dinner, all-hands, or something else?"
2. "How many people are you expecting?"
3. "Do you have a date in mind, or are you still planning?"
4. "Any dietary restrictions I should know about — vegan, gluten-free, allergies?"
5. "What's your rough budget per person — are you thinking a standard spread or premium with upgraded proteins?"

**The Close:**
> "Perfect — based on what you've told me, I'd recommend [OPTION]. That comes out to about $[PRICE] total for [GUEST COUNT] guests. I can hold a delivery slot for you if you want to move forward today — what do you think?"

**If They're Not Ready:**
> "No problem at all — let me send you our chef menus and a custom quote by end of day. What's the best email? And if it looks good, just text me at this number and I'll lock in your slot."

---

### Outbound Cold Call Script

**Open:**
> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Culinary Block Collective. I know this is out of the blue — do you have 45 seconds?"

*(Wait for yes)*

> "We're a corporate catering kitchen in San Jose — we have in-house chefs, everything from sushi/Japanese and Chinese to Mexican fusion and BBQ. We work with a lot of tech teams in [their city], usually for team lunches and product launches. Do you ever use outside caterers for your office events?"

**If Yes:** "Great — what does that usually look like for you? How many people, how often?" → Go to discovery questions.

**If No:** "That's totally fine — mind if I send over our menu just in case? Some teams switch when they see the variety we offer. Best email?"

---

### Text Message Templates

**First Text to a Warm Lead (LinkedIn → Text):**
> "Hi [Name]! It's [Your Name] from Culinary Block — we connected on LinkedIn. We do diverse chef catering for tech teams in the Bay Area ($25–$40/person, Chef's Spread format, 50-guest minimum). Would love to send over our menus. Mind if I text them to you?"

**Quote Follow-Up (Sent quote 2 days ago, no reply):**
> "Hey [Name] — just checking in on the quote I sent for [Company]. Happy to adjust the menu or pricing if anything didn't fit. Should I hold that delivery slot or release it?"

**Post-Event Recurring Offer:**
> "Hi [Name]! Hope the team loved the food today. We have a recurring catering program — fixed per-head pricing, rotating menus, 90-day review clause. Want me to put together a standing order proposal for [Company]?"
