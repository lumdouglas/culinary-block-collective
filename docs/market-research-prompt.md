```text
You are a market research analyst specializing in the foodservice and corporate
catering industry. Conduct a comprehensive deep dive into the corporate catering
market in the South Bay Area (San Jose, Santa Clara, Sunnyvale, Mountain View,
Palo Alto, Cupertino, Redwood City, and surrounding cities in Santa Clara and
San Mateo counties).

IMPORTANT SCOPE: We need to understand the market segment we're entering, NOT
the market we can't compete in. Read our business model below before researching.

---

## OUR BUSINESS MODEL — CULINARY BLOCK COLLECTIVE (CBC)

CBC is a corporate catering operation based in San Jose, CA, layered on top of
an existing commercial kitchen commissary (Culinary Block LLC) that houses 20+
independent chef-tenants. CBC is NOT a restaurant and NOT a catering platform —
it is an operator that curates, sells, and delivers catering using the
commissary's roster of independent chefs.

**How it works:**
- CBC handles all client-facing operations: sales, quoting, dietary labeling,
  food safety labels (with consume-by times per FDA Food Code), delivery, and
  invoicing
- Chefs focus on cooking and provide their own serving ware and chafing dishes
- CBC charges the client and collects applicable sales tax; chefs receive their
  agreed payout percentage (sales tax is not deducted from chef payout)
- Commission: 25% CBC / 75% chef (standard); one chef at 20% CBC / 80% chef
  (negotiated exception)

**Service format:** Chef's Spread (self-serve stations with chafing dishes) ONLY
— no individual boxed meals

**Two service tiers:**
- Tier 1 — Drop-Off Spread: CBC delivers, client handles cleanup. No on-site
  staff. $25–$40/person depending on cuisine and tier.
- Tier 2 — Attended Service: On-site CBC staff for setup, station maintenance,
  and breakdown. +$8/person on top of Tier 1 price. This is the margin engine.

**Pricing:** $25–$30/person (Standard) · $40/person (Premium) · +$8/person
(Attended Service). Total range: $25–$48/person.

**Minimums:** 50 guests per order · $1,250–$2,000 minimum order value

**Current chef roster (dynamic — changes with commissary tenants):**
- Chinese cuisine: $25–$30/person
- Mexican Fusion: $30/person (Standard), $40/person (Premium)
- Sushi / Japanese: $40/person (Premium only)
- Coming soon: cold-pressed juice, tacos, Thai — pricing TBD

**Target clients:** Tech companies with 50–300 employees in the South Bay.
Office managers and HR/People Ops directors who book team lunches, all-hands
catering, client dinners, and product launch events.

**Key differentiators:**
- Multi-cuisine diversity from independent specialist chefs under one operation
- Commissary-based = lower overhead than a standalone catering company
- Every item individually labeled for dietary needs (vegan, GF, nut-free, etc.)
- On-time guarantee (10% off next order if food isn't staged and ready)
- Transparent pricing with no hidden fees

**Stage:** Pre-launch. Infrastructure built, no active outreach or paying
clients yet. Break-even target: 9 orders.

**What we need from this research:** Validate our pricing, identify the right
company size to target, understand order frequency and seasonality, map the
competitive landscape we're entering, and surface any risks or opportunities
we haven't considered.

---

## 1. MARKET SIZE & VALUE

- What is the estimated total addressable market (TAM) for corporate catering
  in the South Bay Area / Silicon Valley corridor? Break down by:
  - Total annual spend on corporate catering (all segments)
  - The serviceable addressable market (SAM) for NON-contract, per-event
    catering (exclude companies with full-time managed dining programs like
    Bon Appétit, Compass Group, Sodexo, Aramark)
  - The serviceable obtainable market (SOM) for a small operator targeting
    tech companies with 50–300 employees ordering self-serve spreads at
    $25–$48/person
- How has this market grown or contracted post-2023, factoring in return-to-office
  trends, hybrid work policies, and the shift from daily perks to periodic
  catered events?

## 2. PRICE TIER ANALYSIS

- What percentage of corporate catering orders in this region fall into each
  price band (per person)?
  - Under $15/person (pizza, sandwich trays, fast casual)
  - $15–$20/person (standard boxed lunches, basic buffet)
  - $20–$25/person (elevated casual, ethnic cuisine buffets)
  - $25–$30/person (chef-prepared spreads, diverse menus)
  - $30–$40/person (premium proteins, sushi, elevated presentations)
  - $40–$50/person (premium chef's table, specialty cuisines)
  - $50+/person (fine dining catering, plated service, executive events)
- Which price tiers are growing fastest and why?
- Where is the market oversaturated vs. underserved?
- Is there a "dead zone" price range that's hard to sell — too expensive for
  routine orders but not premium enough for special events?

## 3. ORDER FREQUENCY & PATTERNS

- How often do mid-size tech companies (50–300 employees) order catering?
  Break down by:
  - Weekly recurring (e.g., team lunch Wednesdays)
  - Bi-weekly or monthly all-hands / town halls
  - Quarterly events (board meetings, planning offsites)
  - Ad hoc celebrations (birthdays, milestones, holidays)
- What is the average number of catering orders per month for a company in
  this size range?
- What is the average order value and average headcount per order?
- Is there seasonality? Which months/quarters see peak and trough demand?
- What days of the week are highest volume for catered office meals?

## 4. TARGET COMPANY PROFILE

- What company size (by headcount) is the sweet spot for per-event catering
  (not managed dining)?
  - At what employee count do companies typically switch from per-event
    ordering to a full-time contract dining provider (Bon Appétit, Compass,
    etc.)?
  - What is the typical annual catering budget for companies at 50, 100, 150,
    200, 250, and 300 employees?
  - What percentage of companies in each size band have a dedicated office
    manager or People Ops person responsible for ordering?
- Who are the typical decision-makers and what are their priorities?
  (cost per head, dietary coverage, variety, reliability, ease of ordering)
- What industries beyond tech (biotech, legal, finance, startups) in the
  South Bay order catering at this scale?

## 5. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE (exclude enterprise contract dining)

- Who are the key competitors in the South Bay for per-event corporate catering
  at the $25–$50/person range? Include:
  - Catering-specific companies (local caterers, chef collectives)
  - Platforms and marketplaces (ezCater, ZeroCater, Fooda, CaterCow, Grubhub
    Corporate)
  - Restaurants that also cater (high-end ethnic restaurants, farm-to-table)
  - Ghost kitchen / commissary-based caterers
- What is each competitor's positioning, price range, and market share?
- What are common complaints about existing options from corporate buyers?
  (pulled from reviews, Reddit, Glassdoor food perk mentions, etc.)
- What gaps exist that a diverse, chef-collective model could fill?

## 6. BUYER BEHAVIOR & DECISION DRIVERS

- How do corporate buyers currently discover and select caterers?
  (Google, referrals, platforms like ezCater, direct sales, social media)
- What are the top 5 decision factors ranked by importance?
- How important is cuisine diversity / rotation in a single vendor vs.
  ordering from multiple vendors?
- What role do dietary accommodations (vegan, gluten-free, halal, kosher)
  play in vendor selection?
- What is the typical lead time for ordering? (same-day, 2–3 days, 1 week+)
- Do buyers prefer a single point of contact / account manager or self-serve
  online ordering?

## 7. MARKET TRENDS & TAILWINDS

- How is the return-to-office movement affecting catering demand in 2025–2026?
- Are companies shifting from daily food perks to periodic high-quality catered
  events? What does this mean for per-event caterers?
- How is the "experience over frequency" trend affecting price sensitivity?
  (i.e., companies spending more per event but ordering less often)
- What role does sustainability, local sourcing, and supporting small businesses
  play in procurement decisions?
- Are there regulatory or health department trends in California affecting
  catering operations (food safety labeling, cottage food law changes, etc.)?

## 8. UNIT ECONOMICS BENCHMARKS

- What are typical gross margins for catering operations at this price point
  and scale?
- What is the industry-standard commission or markup when a platform/operator
  sits between the chef and the client?
- What is the customer acquisition cost (CAC) for landing a new corporate
  catering account through cold outreach vs. platform vs. referral?
- What is the average customer lifetime value (LTV) for a recurring corporate
  account ordering 2–4x per month?
- What is the typical churn rate for corporate catering accounts?

## 9. RISKS & BARRIERS

- What are the biggest barriers to entry for a small catering collective
  in this market?
- What are the most common reasons corporate catering startups fail in this
  segment?
- What regulatory/licensing requirements exist in Santa Clara and San Mateo
  counties specifically for catering delivery from a commissary kitchen?

---

FORMAT: Present findings with specific numbers, ranges, and sources wherever
possible. Where hard data isn't available, provide informed estimates with
confidence levels (high/medium/low) and explain your reasoning. Use tables
for comparative data. Flag any data points that are pre-2024 so I can weight
them accordingly.

At the end, include a section called "IMPLICATIONS FOR CBC" that synthesizes
the 5 most important strategic takeaways for a commissary-based chef collective
entering this market at $25–$48/person targeting 50–300 employee tech companies
in the South Bay.
```
