## What is CBC?

Culinary Block Collective is a corporate catering operation built on top of the Culinary Block commissary. I'm using the kitchen infrastructure we already share to sell your food to Bay Area tech companies — and I want you to be part of it.

The companies I'm targeting are 50–300 person tech firms in the South Bay and Peninsula. Office managers and HR teams who need reliable, high-quality catering for team lunches, offsites, and recurring weekly orders. This is a market that has money and orders consistently. They just need a trusted vendor who shows up on time with great food.

That's where you come in.

## How the Money Works

**Client pays $20–$30 per box.** You receive **75%** of every dollar collected.

| Box Tier | Client Price | Your Payout (75%) | CBC Keeps (25%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Budget | $20/box | $15.00 | $5.00 |
| Core | $25/box | $18.75 | $6.25 |
| Premium | $30/box | $22.50 | $7.50 |

**Minimum order: 50 boxes.**

So a single 50-box order at the Core tier = **$937.50 to you** for one prep session.

A recurring weekly order of 75 boxes at Core tier = **$1,406/week** — before you've done any selling, any invoicing, or any client management.

My 25% covers: sales operations, client relationships, branding, website, coordination, and logistics support. That's the trade.

## Why This Works for You

## Questions You Might Have

**"Why 25%? That seems high."**
Sales, ops, branding, coordination, and logistics aren't free. Most distributors and brokers take 30–40%. I'm taking 25% and doing substantially more work than a distributor — I'm building the client relationships, handling every touchpoint, and guaranteeing you get paid.

**"What if an order gets cancelled?"**
We have a clear policy. If a client cancels 7+ days out, no impact to you. Within 7 days, CBC keeps the deposit — you owe nothing. Within 72 hours, you receive 25% of the order value to cover food costs. Your prep time has real value and the policy protects it.

**"What if the client has dietary requirements or wants customizations?"**
I handle that conversation. If it's outside what you offer, we decline or route to another chef. You never get a surprise requirement day-of.

**"Do I have to take every order you send me?"**
No. You tell me your availability. I route accordingly. If you can't take it, I find another chef or decline the order.

**"What about my existing customers?"**
Nothing changes. This is additive — new clients, new orders, separate from whatever you're doing now.
