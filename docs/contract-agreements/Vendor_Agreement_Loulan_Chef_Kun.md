This Agreement sets out the terms under which the Contractor provides catering services through Culinary Block Collective ("CBC") to CBC's corporate clients.

---

## 1. Services

**Contractor is responsible for:**
- Preparing Chinese and Southeast Asian cuisine in Chef's Spread (self-serve station) format. No individual boxed meals.
- Preparing and packaging all food items, clearly identifying each item by name, and having all items ready for CBC pickup at the agreed time.
- Meeting any dietary requirements (vegan, gluten-free, etc.) listed on the confirmed order.
- Following all applicable California food safety and health regulations.
- Providing all serving equipment — chafing dishes, platters, serving utensils, and spread materials.
- Keeping food at safe temperatures from preparation through CBC pickup.

**CBC is responsible for:**
- Picking up all food from Contractor's kitchen at the agreed time and delivering it to the client site.
- Affixing dietary labels and food safety labels (including consume-by times) to all food items before delivery.
- Setting up the Chef's Spread at the client site, including chafing dishes, printed menu cards, and dietary breakdown sheets.
- Sales, marketing, and client acquisition.
- Sending the Contractor complete event details (guest count, location, dietary needs, pickup time) at least 48 hours before the event.
- Invoicing, deposit collection, and all client billing. CBC is the sole billing entity.
- Obtaining written client acknowledgment of food safety labeling before each event.

## 3. Menu

- Each order is based on a 5-item set chosen from Contractor's available menu. White rice is included. Brown rice is available as an upgrade at +$1.00 per person.
- Contractor may offer additional items or upsells. All add-on pricing must be submitted to CBC in writing before CBC includes them in a quote.
- Menu changes require CBC's written approval with at least seven (7) days' notice, unless no confirmed orders are affected.
- Contractor may update available dates and menu options at any time, as long as no confirmed orders are affected.

---

## 4. Payment

**Contractor's price:**

| **Item** | **Rate** |
|---|---|
| Chef's Spread (per person) | $18.75 |
| Brown Rice Upgrade (per person) | +$1.00 |

- Contractor's payout for each event: $18.75 × confirmed guest count, plus any approved add-ons.
- CBC pays Contractor within 30 days of the event (Net 30).
- CBC absorbs all credit card processing fees. These are not deducted from Contractor's payout.
- CBC collects and remits all applicable sales tax. Sales tax is not deducted from Contractor's payout.
- Contractor is responsible for their own income taxes, insurance, and benefits.

**Pricing changes:**
Either party may propose a price change. Any change must be agreed to in writing and signed by both parties before it takes effect.

---

## 5. Tastings

- Contractor will make tastings available up to once per week, by appointment through CBC.
- CBC handles all scheduling and client communication for tastings.
- CBC reimburses Contractor for food costs from tastings. Contractor submits an itemized receipt within 7 days of the tasting. CBC includes reimbursement in the next payment cycle.

---

## 6. Cancellations

**If a client cancels:**

| **When** | **Contractor's outcome** |
|---|---|
| 7 or more days before the event | No payment. No obligation. |
| Less than 7 days but more than 72 hours before | No payment. No obligation. |
| Within 72 hours of the event | Contractor receives 25% of the total food order value to cover ingredient costs already incurred. |

For 72-hour cancellations, CBC retains the client deposit and invoices the client separately for Contractor's 25% reimbursement.

**Late delivery:**
CBC is responsible for on-time delivery to the client site. If a client credit is issued due to a late delivery caused by CBC, the cost is absorbed by CBC and is not deducted from Contractor's payout. If a late delivery was caused by Contractor failing to have food ready at the agreed pickup time, CBC will notify Contractor in writing within 48 hours. Contractor may dispute in writing within 7 days.

**If Contractor cancels:**
- Contractor must give at least 7 days' notice.
- Cancellation within 7 days of a confirmed order: Contractor pays CBC a $250 fee within 14 days.

---

## 7. Food Safety

- Contractor is responsible for food safety from preparation through the point of CBC pickup.
- All food must be packed in food-safe, temperature-appropriate containers and clearly labeled with item names at pickup.
- Contractor must maintain safe food temperatures through pickup. CBC is responsible for safe transport and timely delivery from that point.
- CBC affixes dietary labels and food safety labels (including consume-by times) before delivery. Consume-by times follow California food safety rules and the FDA Food Code: ready-to-eat food held outside safe temperature range (below 41°F or above 135°F) must be consumed or discarded within 4 hours.
- CBC obtains written client acknowledgment of the consume-by policy before each event.

---

## 8. Insurance and Liability

- Contractor is responsible for all claims arising from food preparation, ingredients, allergens, and temperature control through the point of CBC pickup.
- CBC is responsible for all claims arising from delivery, labeling, setup, and its sales, marketing, and client communications.
- Contractor must carry product liability and general liability insurance of at least $1,000,000 each.
- CBC must carry general liability insurance of at least $1,000,000 for its own activities.
- CBC may inspect Contractor's kitchen and records at reasonable times with advance notice.
- CBC may terminate this Agreement immediately if Contractor fails to meet food safety requirements.

---

## 9. Indemnification

- Contractor will defend and hold CBC harmless from any claims arising from Contractor's food preparation, ingredients, allergens, or temperature control through pickup — unless CBC caused the issue.
- CBC will defend and hold Contractor harmless from any claims arising from CBC's delivery, labeling, setup, sales, marketing, or client communications — unless Contractor caused the issue.

---

## 10. Confidentiality and Non-Solicitation

- Confidential Information includes client lists, contact details, pricing, order volumes, and business strategies.
- Contractor will not use Confidential Information for any purpose outside of performing services under this Agreement.
- For 12 months after this Agreement ends: if Contractor directly provides catering services to a client that CBC first introduced, using contact information or details obtained through this Agreement, Contractor will pay CBC 15% of revenue earned from those services during that period.
- This does not prevent Contractor from serving clients they found on their own, running their existing catering business, or marketing their services generally.

---

## 11. Intellectual Property

Menus, recipes, and food creations belong to the Contractor. CBC may use photos and video of Contractor's food from CBC events for marketing, with Contractor's permission. Contractor will not unreasonably withhold permission.

---

## 12. Term and Termination

- This Agreement starts on the date signed and continues until either party ends it with 30 days' written notice, provided no confirmed orders are pending.
- On termination, Contractor must complete all confirmed orders or pay the applicable cancellation fee.
- Either party may end the Agreement immediately for a serious breach. For non-safety breaches, the breaching party has 10 days to fix the issue before termination takes effect.

---

## 13. Independent Contractor

Contractor is an independent contractor — not an employee, partner, or agent of CBC. Contractor may use subcontractors but remains fully responsible for their work. Nothing in this Agreement creates an employment relationship or entitles Contractor to employee benefits.

---

## 14. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by California law. Any dispute will first go to good-faith mediation in San Jose, California. If mediation fails, disputes will be resolved by binding arbitration under California law.

---

## 15. Entire Agreement

This is the complete agreement between the parties. It replaces all prior discussions, representations, or agreements on this subject. Any changes must be in writing and signed by both parties.
