Meet Chef Kun

Cruise Ship Kitchens. Silk Road Flavors.

"Consistent, high-quality meals tailored for corporate dining."

Chef Kun is a seasoned culinary professional with international experience behind him — including a role as Assistant Executive Chef with Norwegian Cruise Line, where feeding thousands of guests daily across diverse global cuisines is just Tuesday. He brings that same infrastructure thinking to every corporate spread: no chaos, no shortcuts, just reliable execution at scale.

"His approach combines authentic flavors with efficient execution, ensuring every meal is both satisfying and reliable for teams of all sizes."

Under his brand Loulan — named after the legendary ancient Silk Road city that once connected China to the world — Chef Kun offers two distinct cuisine styles: Chinese and Southeast Asian. Both menus are built from his deep experience with regional cooking, not approximations. The cumin beef is Xinjiang. The rendang is Malaysian. The three cup chicken is Taiwanese. He doesn't flatten the world into a single "Asian" category.

Chef Kun operates out of the Culinary Block commissary in the Bay Area, and his food is delivered, labeled, and staged by the CBC team. Every item comes with individual dietary labels. Every spread is ready before your team walks in.
